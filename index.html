  4. I chose to follow the tutorial on file organization and naming conventions. The biggest takeaway from this tutorial is that it's important to pick a naming convention and consistently stick to it so that you can find things efficiently later. General advice from the tutorial includes using all lowercase when naming files (because the internet is case-sensitive!), using hyphens or underscores instead of spaces, avoiding abbreviations, and using version numbers/dates to practice good iterative design. The tutorial gives a few examples of popular naming conventions, but seems to subtly recommend snake_case and kebab-case as the best conventions, since those are the two that follow the suggestions outlined in the rest of the tutorial.

Projects of Interest

The overarching theme of the projects below were that they are media forms of some things I have been pondering on recently. Generally, I tend to like stuff that calls attention to things that we wouldn't normally question.
  1. It is as if You Were Doing Paperwork: This project was remarkable to me because it made me consider just how redundant and pointless certain everyday tasks (like paperwork) truly are. I think normally people tend to not question certain status quo facets of life (like going to work and doing paperwork), so this project could inspire people to look for more out of their lives.
  2. Learning to Love You More: This project intrigued me for reasons opposite to the "Paperwork" project above. I like how it encourages people to romanticize parts of their daily life through a series of prompts. Sometimes people forget about the little things that make life so great, so this was a cool project to see.
  3. Data Will Help Us: The best way I can describe this project is "vaguely menacing." It exposes the dark side of our relatively newfound obsession with data and suggests that humans haven't tapped into their hearts as much as we have tapped into this newfound data obsession. I also find the choice of typeface and font color to be intriguing, and I wonder why they chose it.
